With proper farm culture, a typical maize farm yields 5 to 10 bags (100 kg per bag) of maize per acre. Soybeans are one of the world's most popular and versatile food crops. They are processed into a variety of food products, such as soy protein, tofu, soybean oil, soy sauce, miso, natto, and tempeh. Soybeans are also eaten whole, including in the form of immature soybeans known as edamame. In sub-Saharan Africa (SSA), soya beans are a non-staple crop that is predominantly used as livestock feed. But due to its wide range of uses worldwide, soya production is gradually attaining commercial status as more producers become aware of the market's availability. Soya beans are mostly cultivated for export in Ghana and other West African countries.

Groundnut is a multipurpose legume crop widely cultivated in sub-Saharan Africa (SSA).  The average yield of groundnuts per acre is estimated to be between 8 and 15 bags ( 100 kg per bag) per acre. In Ghana, the savannah zone  is predominately the agro-ecological zone growing groundnut, which accounts for about 94% of the annual groundnut production in the county. A typical groundnut crop can be harvested between 4 and 5 months after planting. Groundnut seed can be consumed raw (non-heated), boiled, roasted, or used to make confections. It is also used to make edible groundnut oil, and its flour and paste are also used to make assorted food products. The groundnut shells and stalks constitute nutritious fodder for livestock.

Around 10 - 15 million people in West Africa cultivate rice as their primary source of food. These people live in societies dotted along the coast, from Senegal's Casamance to the Ivory Coast's Bandama River bend. In Ghana's economy, where it is grown for both food and profit, rice is a crucial strategic crop. Due to changes in consumer behavior, urbanization, and population growth, rice consumption is still rising. According to MoFA statistics, paddy rice production between 2008 and 2020 ranged between 302,000 MT and 987,000 MT (181,000 to 622,000 MT of milled rice), with significant annual fluctuations.
